Welcome to My Portfolio!

Tēnā koutou katoa! My name is Isaac Karota and welcome to my online teacher portfolio. This space is designed to showcase my journey, philosophy, and practical applications in teaching. I believe in fostering a dynamic, inclusive, and culturally responsive learning environment where every student can thrive.

Explore my sections above to learn more about my commitment to Te Tiriti o Waitangi, my ongoing professional growth, the relationships I build within the educational community, and my diverse approaches to teaching and learning.

1. Te Tiriti o Waitangi & Partnership

This section will showcase how I understand Te Tiriti o Waitangi and how it forms the foundation of my teaching. You'll also see how I actively build true companionship with Māori students, their families (whānau), and the wider community.

My Commitment to Te Tiriti

My New Zealand educational journey is deeply informed by Te Tiriti o Waitangi. I am committed to creating a bicultural learning environment that values Māori language, culture, and identity, ensuring equitable and culturally responsive outcomes for all students. I will continuously reflect on how to honour Māori aspirations and knowledge systems within my teaching.

Te Tiriti & Partnership in Action:

Whānau Hui

Captured during our annual whānau hui, this image reflects the vibrant discussions and shared decision-making process with our Māori families. These meetings are crucial for aligning home and school aspirations, fostering a strong sense of community and collective responsibility in our learning environment, and ensuring cultural values are respected.

Kapa Haka Performance

Students proudly performing kapa haka, showcasing their cultural identity and teamwork. This initiative encourages engagement with te reo Māori and tikanga, building confidence and fostering a sense of belonging within our school. It’s a powerful expression of bicultural pride.

Collaborative Inquiry with Iwi

Working alongside local iwi representatives to design curriculum content around local history and significant sites. This partnership ensures authentic learning experiences that are culturally relevant and respectful, deepening student connection to their rohe (area) and its heritage.

3. Professional Relationships

Collaborations / Exaluations with Colleagues

I actively contribute to a positive and supportive school culture. I frequently engage in co-planning sessions, share resources, and provide constructive feedback to peers, fostering a collaborative learning environment amongst Henderson High School staff.

Community Garden Project

Students and community volunteers working together in our school garden, integrating mātauranga Māori (Māori knowledge) about sustainable practices and kai (food) cultivation. This project builds practical skills while reinforcing community partnership and environmental kaitiakitanga (guardianship).
